Absys
||||||
Absys was an early declarative programming language from the University of Aberdeen.  It anticipated a number of features of Prolog such as negation as failure, aggregation operators, the 
central role of backtracking and constraint solving.  Absys was the first implementation of a logic programming language.The name Absys was chosen as an abbreviation for Aberdeen System.
