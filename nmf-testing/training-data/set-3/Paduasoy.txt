Paduasoy
||||||
Paduasoy or padesoy (; French: peau de soie) is a luxurious strong corded or grosgrain silk textile that originated in Early Modern Europe. The term paduasoy first appeared in English in 1663.Paduasoy silk was woven in a variation of the satin weave, with bindings arranged to create fine cross-ridges across the fabric.The original French term, peau de soie, is once again part of the English vernacular.
