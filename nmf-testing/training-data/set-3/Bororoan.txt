Bororoan languages
||||||
The Borôroan languages of Brazil are Borôro and the extinct Umotína and Otuke. They are sometimes considered to form part of the proposed Macro-Jê language family,: 547  though this has been disputed.: 64–8 They are called the Borotuke languages by Mason (1950), a portmanteau of Bororo and Otuke.
