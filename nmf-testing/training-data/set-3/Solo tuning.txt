Solo tuning
||||||
Solo tuning is a system of choosing the reeds for a diatonic wind instrument (such as a harmonica or accordion) to fit a pattern where blow notes repeat a sequence of

C E G C(perhaps shifted to begin with E or with G) and draw notes follow a repeating sequence of

D F A B(perhaps correspondingly shifted).  Or, alternately, these blow notes and draw notes, raised by a semitone, to

C♯ F G♯ C♯and to

D♯ F♯ A♯ CTraditionally, this tuning is used with chromatic harmonicas, as opposed to the more common and popular diatonic harmonicas, which use Richter tuning.
